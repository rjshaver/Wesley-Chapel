Rev. Catherine N. Davis

Minister at Wesley Chapel United Methodist Church

Rev. Davis has been at Wesley Chapel since 2008.  Prior to coming to Stanly county, she served Center UMC in Yadkinville (2006-08), Union Ridge UMC in Winston Salem (2002-06), and the Creston-Green Valley Charge in Ashe County (2000-02).  She was ordained into Full Connection as an Elder in 2009 at Annual Conference.

 

Cathy loves being at Wesley Chapel and sees only the potential that God has given the church to grow in Spirit and in people as they reach out to the community around Richfield and Misenheimer.  She is a firm believer in offering people Jesus by showing them that you care, being compassionate, and mission-minded in your community.  It is important however, that we teach people to fish rather than just giving away fish.  Plus, we must offer the living water that only Jesus Christ can provide.  People all over are thirsting for something in life that “things” cannot give them.  Jesus holds the answer and offers himself freely to all. 

 

 

Brad Kenn

Mr. Kenn has been recently hired as our new part-time assistant Youth Pastor.  He is from Maryvale, AZ and graduated from Desert Edge High School in 2008.  He currently attends Pfeiffer University and is a Youth Ministry major, hoping to graduate in 2012.  We first met Brad when he and his friend Aaron Duncan, got us involved in Relay for Life at Pfeiffer.  Brad loves to work with young people and hopes to help our Body Builders expand into the community reaching unchurched youth.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


Dr. Jean Raines

Professor of Music, Pfeiffer University

Organist/Choir Director, Wesley Chapel United Methodist Church

Dr. Raines joined the faculty of Pfeiffer University in 1980 and she has been organist/choir director at Wesley Chapel since 1981. She also teaches private piano and woodwind lessons to elementary through high school aged students  in the community. Dr. Raines holds the Bachelor of Science in Education degree with certification in music and mathematics from the University of Memphis, the Master of Music Education degree from the University of Memphis, and the Ph.D. in applied clarinet-theory-literature from Michigan State University.
