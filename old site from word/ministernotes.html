John 20: 1-18 The Message

 1-2 Early in the morning on the first day of the week, while it was still dark, Mary Magdalene came to the tomb and saw that the stone was moved away from the entrance. She ran at once to Simon Peter and the other disciple, the one Jesus loved, breathlessly panting, "They took the Master from the tomb. We don't know where they've put him."

 3-10Peter and the other disciple left immediately for the tomb. They ran, neck and neck. The other disciple got to the tomb first, outrunning Peter. Stooping to look in, he saw the pieces of linen cloth lying there, but he didn't go in. Simon Peter arrived after him, entered the tomb, observed the linen cloths lying there, and the kerchief used to cover his head not lying with the linen cloths but separate, neatly folded by itself. Then the other disciple, the one who had gotten there first, went into the tomb, took one look at the evidence, and believed. No one yet knew from the Scripture that he had to rise from the dead. The disciples then went back home.

 11-13But Mary stood outside the tomb weeping. As she wept, she knelt to look into the tomb and saw two angels sitting there, dressed in white, one at the head, the other at the foot of where Jesus' body had been laid. They said to her, "Woman, why do you weep?"

 13-14"They took my Master," she said, "and I don't know where they put him." After she said this, she turned away and saw Jesus standing there. But she didn't recognize him.

 15Jesus spoke to her, "Woman, why do you weep? Who are you looking for?"

   She, thinking that he was the gardener, said, "Mister, if you took him, tell me where you put him so I can care for him."

 16Jesus said, "Mary."

   Turning to face him, she said in Hebrew, "Rabboni!" meaning "Teacher!"

 17Jesus said, "Don't cling to me, for I have not yet ascended to the Father. Go to my brothers and tell them, 'I ascend to my Father and your Father, my God and your God.'"

 18Mary Magdalene went, telling the news to the disciples: "I saw the Master!" And she told them everything he said to her.

*************************************************************************************

He’s alive!  He has risen, just as he said he would!  I’ve often thought of how happy I would have been had I been the first to arrive at the empty tomb early on that Sabbath morning.  To find that my Lord was gone, that what he had said about his resurrection on the third day, about him going to be with the Father, that all of it, everything he said had come true.  Wow!  He’s alive! 

But we all know that isn’t at all what happened, is it?  Those who came to the tomb that morning, at the  crack of dawn, were expecting to find a dead man.  I mean, isn’t that why you come to a cemetery, to visit the dead?  The women brought their spices to anoint a dead body.  And so when they discovered that the body was missing, well, quite frankly I believe they were in shock.

After all, it had been a shocking few days.  So much turmoil politically.  Maybe that’s who had taken the body.  So much turmoil within the disciples.  Could they have taken him?

So, Mary Magdelene runs to Simon Peter and John and tells them that the body of Jesus is gone from the tomb.  She doesn’t say with joy – he is gone!  He is risen just as he said he would!  No, she says, “They have taken the Lord out of the tomb, as we don’t know where they have laid him.”

I guess it never really struck me that the first Easter morning was so full of shock.  I guess I too would have been shocked to think someone had taken my beloved teacher’s body.  I would have been shocked that someone could have rolled the heavy stone away from the tomb.  I may have even been shocked that the Roman authorities might think it was me or my friends who took the body.

Shock.  We’ve all experienced it before.  Remember how you felt on 9/11.  Shock.  Remember how you felt when you heard that a neighbor or friend had died?  Shock.  Remember how you felt when you found out that you or someone in your immediate family were sick and needed treatment?  Shock.  I remember when I got the diagnosis of a chronic illness that I would have to have treatments for over the rest of my life.  Shock. 

So what then do you do when the shock wears off?  Sure, for awhile all the followers of Jesus were in shock.  Shock that he was being murdered, crucified for something that he didn’t do!  Shock that he was dead – after all, many had watched this man who was just beginning to come into power in their eyes die an early death on the cross.  Shock, that now his body was missing.  Would they stay in shock?    

It’s a funny thing about life.  Life doesn’t allow us to stay in shock for very long and I believe that is a good thing.  Once we have experienced the initial shock of a situation, or in this case several situations at once, we then have to make a choice – we can either give in to the shock and let it rule our lives with depression and panic and anxiety and worry, or we can choose to keep on living life to its fullest.

For a while, I believe the disciples allowed shock to rule their lives and so they lived in panic and depression and worry.  Even after Jesus had appeared to Mary and told her he had risen and was going to be with God, they didn’t believe her.  Shock had control of them.  They were living as though Jesus was dead.  They were living as though they could be next.  They didn’t understand that because of Jesus the resurrection was meant for them too.  How could they?  They were still living as though he was a dead man.

But Mary was persistent – or at least I think she must have been.  The Gospel of Luke tells us that all of the women were persistent.  They understood Jesus’ previous words that he would rise from the dead.  Their shock had worn off.  But the men, still in shock.

Somewhere along the way, over the course of time, hearing the women insist that Jesus was alive, the shock began to wear off.  And it was then that the disciples began to live again.  It was then that they began to live as though Jesus was alive.  Not only were they choosing to live life to its fullest, they were proclaiming that Jesus was alive!  Not a ghost, and therefore not a man – the very Son of God!  Not immediately recognizable to even his closest followers.  The wounds on his body, by which we are healed, remain on him even after the resurrection.  He seems the same, but different.

Jesus’ resurrection is much more than a dead man made alive again.  It is much different than his raising of Lazarus who was destined to die again even after being raised by the Christ.  Resurrection is more than just dead people walking around!  Resurrection points to an inbreaking of God’s rule over everything that alienates itself from God. 

God has in the resurrection of Jesus, decided to offer all of us a way to get right with him again.  A way that we can come to God directly, confessing our sins and turning away from them, receiving forgiveness and grace as we live life to its fullest in the love of the Son, Jesus the Christ.  And so, it is in this glorious resurrection that we can not only find new life now, but new life eternally with our creator.

He is risen!  He is alive!  But do we really live like we believe that?  Or are we more like the disciples right after the tombstone was moved – in shock still?  Worrying about tomorrow.  Fretting over things we cannot control.  Afraid we will get in trouble or bother someone if we share with them what Jesus means to us. 

HOW ARE WE LIVING?  He is alive!  In each heart here today!  The time is now that we begin to live as though Jesus IS alive, as though we are resurrection people, full of hope and new life, joy in the midst of trials, love for those who are unloveable, acceptance for those the world says we should not accept, working together to make changes but respecting one another in our diversity, proclaiming peace instead of prosperity, grace instead of guns, forgiveness instead of fighting, mercy instead of giving people what they deserve. 

Thank God that through Jesus the Messiah, we were not given what we deserved!  But instead we’ve been given new life!  Instead we’ve been offered resurrection from the pit of depression and anxiety and worry and panic that encompasses the shocks of life.  Praise God that because Jesus lives, we too can really begin to live…to live as though he lives.
